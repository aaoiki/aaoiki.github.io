Programmer Tools: Dotfiles Development


April 6, 2016


We programmers are lazy. We make frameworks, we make CLI tools, we make methods and call them helpers, and in the realm of being lazy, we package our configuration files and bootstrap them with a simple command so we can run them anywhere and that’s called dotfiles!

What are they?

First of all what is a dotfile? A dotfile is a file that starts with a dot/period and hence the name and that how hidden files are made in Unix-like systems. If you are coming from a Unix-like system, you will find some dotfiles in your home directory by doing an $ ls -a ~. They are mostly configuration files. You can view the them in Ubuntu file manager by pressing Ctrl+H.

Up to the Point

I’ve had my dotfiles for quite some time. I refactored them every once in a while. However, My shell was pretty slow on startup when I used Oh-My-Zsh! which was awesome but a bit slow. I then converted to Prezto. It was convenient for me but still it had many things I never used. I ended up writing my Zsh configuration from scratch.

Whether you think Dotfiles Are Meant to Be Forked or they should be created from scratch, it’s all about personal taste and needs.

Good Tools Do Not Make a Good Carpenter

After I had done a lot of changes and rewrote almost everything, I kind of became obsessed with my dotfiles and how I structure them instead of actually getting the real stuff done. So if you are at the same stage, please take care not to fall into that slope.

كَيْفَ يَطلُبُ العِلْمَ مَن لَهُ حِمَار؟

There’s a story told about Al-Shafi‘i, a Muslim scholar, jurist, and poet who lived in the 8th century, that he used to walk for a long time to get to his lectures, he then said: “How can I study if I don’t have a ride?” and bought a donkey. After a while he became very busy taking care of his donkey and then said: “How can I study when I have a donkey?!”
