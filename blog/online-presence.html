Online Presence Between Socialization and Paranoia Rants

August 25, 2017

Two years ago I deleted almost all my accounts on all major social media platforms. I also took down my blog. I went completely invisible. Ever since then I haven’t used my full name nor my photo on any of my accounts (well, except on LinkedIn).

Although I’m very well aware that there’s no privacy on the internet, this was my paranoid trial to disappear on the internet and only interact anonymosuly. True story: I sometimes receive messages on LinkedIn from friends and classmates since I’m almost unreachable on other platforms.

Buy why would I want to disappear? Well, for starters, I do not believe that myself or anyone should share every detail of their lives online. If we overlook stalking and cyberbullying, we cannot forget about advertising companies collecting and selling our personal data here and there every [REDACTED] single second.

In a world of rising political unrest, governments are increasing their internet moniroting.

Digitizing social/human relationships is bullshit!!

why the lucky stiff or _why was a famous programmer in the Ruby community. You probably heard about him for his many open source contributions or through his unusually artistic book, why’s (poignant) Guide to Ruby. He vanished from the internet on 19 Aug, 2009.

This level of paranoia I’d like to call “The Stallman model” referring to Richard Stallman who is known for his, sometimes, extreme privacy concerns and opinions.

However, I’ve always had this dichotomy between having a less noisy, more private internet browsing and being [virtually] socially active with friends, family, coworkers, etc. Besides, being a programmer, there’s always this need for self-marketing and communincation with the programming community as a real person.
